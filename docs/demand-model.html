Demand Thesis

Why This Works

FIFA does not monetize local activation. The host city and its venue ecosystem sit on a massive inventory of intent-rich, culturally-segmented demand — and no official platform captures it.

01

Cultural Segmentation

Every visiting team brings a distinct language, cuisine, and hospitality profile. The platform knows who is in town and what they want — before they ask.

02

Intent-Driven Matching

Fan intent (food, transport, nightlife, medical, directions) is classified in real time and matched to sponsor offers — not random ads, contextual value.

03

No Rights Conflict

The platform operates exclusively in the non-official, host-city, and local-partner lane. Zero overlap with FIFA's commercial program. Clean monetization.

04

Multi-Channel Delivery

Web PWA, SMS, voice, map cards, rewards — sponsors reach fans on every surface the platform touches, in the fan's own language.

Revenue Lanes

What Gets Monetized

Six categories of local activation inventory that the platform monetizes without touching FIFA's commercial rights.

Cultural Dining

Restaurant partnerships matched to visiting-team cuisine profiles. Reservations, coupons, cultural discovery routes.

Transport

MARTA passes, rideshare coupons, airport transfers, parking offers. Language-aware routing and match-day scheduling.

Hospitality

Hotel packages, premium experiences, VIP lounge access, post-match city activations. Upsell at every touchpoint.

Coupons & Offers

Language-specific sponsor coupons triggered by intent and cultural match. QR redemption with attribution tracking.

Location Triggers

Sponsor placements that fire by zone: gate approach, fan festival, transit hub, concession zone, neighborhood.

Rewards & Passport

Points, badges, challenges, and scavenger hunts tied to sponsor venues. Gamified engagement with conversion tracking.
