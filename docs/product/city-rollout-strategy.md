# Tap FIFA — City Rollout Strategy

## Objective
Move from one successful activation to repeatable multi-city deployment.

## Phase 1 — Proof (Single Venue)
Duration: 2-4 weeks

Goals:
- Validate scan-to-value user flow
- Establish sponsor conversion baseline
- Confirm support fallback reliability

Deliverables:
- 5 poster route campaign
- KPI baseline report
- Operator and sponsor testimonial capture

## Phase 2 — Expansion (Host City)
Duration: 1-2 event windows

Goals:
- Expand to multiple high-traffic zones
- Add more sponsors and reward campaigns
- Introduce language-specific optimization cycles

Deliverables:
- Multi-zone poster map
- Sponsor package ladder in market
- Weekly optimization reports

## Phase 3 — Program (Multi-City)
Duration: seasonal program

Goals:
- Standardize launch playbook across cities
- Reuse creative and package templates
- Build portfolio-level sponsor operations

Deliverables:
- Repeatable deployment checklist
- Central reporting layer across cities
- Portfolio-level pricing and contracts

## City Selection Criteria
Prioritize cities with:
- High international attendance
- Complex venue navigation needs
- Existing sponsor demand for measurable activations
- Local partner ecosystem (hospitality, transport, service ops)

## Operating Model
- Central platform team: product, analytics, sponsor operations
- Local activation team: venue placement, partner coordination, QA
- Event-time command rhythm: daily KPI review and campaign tuning

## Risk Controls
- Fallback path always visible (`1-888-827-3432`)
- Emergency route never blocked by sponsor elements
- Deployment freeze window before event day
- Strict post-event retrospective and patch cycle

## Expansion Trigger Thresholds
Promote to next phase when all are met:
- Stable technical operation across event window
- Sponsor conversion above agreed baseline
- Positive operator feedback on guest flow
- No critical safety/availability incidents
