# Tap FIFA — Client Pitch Deck (Draft)

## Slide 1 — Title
**Tap FIFA: One Scan Guest System**

Tagline:
One scan, one number, one multilingual guest system.

Subhead:
Physical-to-digital fan activation for FIFA-scale events.

## Slide 2 — The Problem
Global events fail at the same moments:
- Guests cannot quickly find what they need.
- Static signage breaks across language barriers.
- Support queues become overloaded during spikes.
- Sponsors get impressions without measurable utility outcomes.

## Slide 3 — The Product
Tap FIFA is a physical-to-digital guest flow:
1. Poster QR scan
2. Instant route-specific page (`/tap/*`)
3. Map + next-step + top suggestions
4. Sponsor/reward placement tied to intent
5. Emergency + phone fallback

## Slide 4 — Why It Works
- Utility-first UX drives adoption with no onboarding friction.
- Multilingual interaction starts immediately from scan.
- Sponsor value is embedded in useful outcomes.
- Fallback channel (`1-888-827-3432`) preserves trust under stress.

## Slide 5 — Demo Flow (30–45 sec)
1. Show poster
2. Scan QR
3. Show page load
4. Show route line and destination marker
5. Show top 3 suggestions + sponsor card
6. Show Help flow + call/SMS fallback

## Slide 6 — Activation Surfaces
Poster set:
- Directions
- Food
- Rewards
- Help
- VIP

Live routes:
- `/tap/directions?zone=stadium_gate_c`
- `/tap/food`
- `/tap/rewards`
- `/tap/help`
- `/tap/vip`

## Slide 7 — Monetization Loop
Physical -> Digital -> Revenue:
1. Scan event
2. Intent classified
3. Sponsor eligibility + ranking
4. Impression/click attribution
5. Reward redemption and repeat engagement

## Slide 8 — Buyer Outcomes
Event operators:
- Better guest throughput
- Fewer wayfinding and support bottlenecks
- Reliable emergency guidance path

Sponsors:
- Intent-aligned placements
- Language-segmented conversion
- Transparent performance reporting

## Slide 9 — Differentiation
Not another event app.
- QR-native entry flow
- Multilingual concierge and map system
- Deterministic support fallback
- Reward engine attached to movement and intent

## Slide 10 — Commercial Ask
Pilot proposal:
- 1 venue
- 1 event window
- 5 poster campaign
- Sponsor package attached

Pilot success criteria:
- Scan-to-open latency
- Route completion interactions
- Sponsor CTR and redemption
- Help flow completion rate

## Slide 11 — Implementation Timeline
Week 1: Poster placement + launch prep
Week 2: Activation and monitoring
Week 3: Sponsor optimization + reporting
Week 4: Executive readout and expansion decision

## Slide 12 — Close
Sales line:
**Tap FIFA is a physical-to-digital guest system. One scan guides fans through the event in their language and turns every step into sponsor value.**
